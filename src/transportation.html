Transportation

Getting To Taniti

Visitors to Taniti have multiple options for reaching the island, with air travel being the most common. Taniti’s small but functional airport accommodates both small jets and propeller planes, making it an accessible destination for travelers seeking a tropical getaway. For those arriving by sea, a charming small cruise ship docks weekly in Yellow Leaf Bay, offering a picturesque overnight stay. Excitingly, the island is in the process of expanding its airport to welcome larger jets in the near future, promising even greater ease of access for visitors eager to experience the beauty of Taniti.

Getting Around Taniti

Getting around Taniti is simple and convenient, with options to suit every traveler's needs. Public buses operate in Taniti City daily from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m., while private buses connect the rest of the island. Taxis are readily available in Taniti City, and rental cars can be secured from a local agency near the airport for those looking to explore more independently. For a more active experience, bikes and helmets (required by law) can be rented from various vendors. Taniti City itself is flat and highly walkable, making it easy to explore on foot, particularly the charming area around Merriton Landing, a popular spot for tourists.
